Cinematic establishing shot of a modern German auto repair workshop, slow dolly forward at walking pace. Four blue hydraulic lifts in perfect perspective receding into depth, all four occupied with cars at different service stages. Center mid-ground: a car at chest height with hood wide open showing a detailed modern engine bay with visible components — intake manifold, valve cover, wiring harness. Right background: a car on a wheel alignment rack with blue clamp arms locked onto all four wheels, alignment monitor displaying colorful abstract measurement data. The entire workshop is bathed in even cool-white LED ceiling grid lighting with subtle blue undertones from the lift columns reflecting on the polished light grey concrete floor. Clean organized environment, every surface has equipment — no empty space. Camera movement: slow stabilized gimbal push forward, smooth constant speed, slight (barely perceptible) upward tilt revealing the full height of the lifts. Color grade: cool industrial palette, blue-grey shadows, warm skin tones on the mechanics preserved, subtle teal highlights on car bodywork. Shallow depth of field, focus on the center plane with foreground and background slightly soft. No text, no logos, no visible signage. Aspect ratio 16:9, output 1080p, 24fps, motion blur at 1/50 shutter. The shot establishes scale, capability, and modern professionalism in a single continuous movement.

**Style reference:** High-end automotive service editorial video like Bosch Car Service or ATU promotional material, but more cinematic. Moody but not dark — well-lit, precise, controlled.
